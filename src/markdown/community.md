# Community

Small community. Shared knowledge.

## Built by people who use it

Lunar Linux is developed and maintained by a small community of Linux users, administrators and developers.

Unlike large distributions with thousands of users and multiple support layers, Lunar Linux keeps communication direct. Users, contributors and developers often meet in the same channels and discussions.

In Lunar Linux, community is not separate from development. The people who use the system are often the people who improve it.

> Come as a user.  
> Stay as a contributor.

## Get in touch

- IRC — real-time discussion and support
- Mailing Lists — announcements and development discussions
- Project Infrastructure — repositories, issue tracking and collaboration

## Participation

### Use Lunar Linux

Start with the Daily Build, install the system and explore how it works.

### Share Feedback

Report bugs, suggest improvements and tell us what can be better.

### Contribute

Help with documentation, modules, testing, infrastructure or development.

## Community principles

### Respect

Technical discussions work best when people respect each other.

### Simplicity

Prefer understandable solutions and clear explanations.

### Curiosity

Questions are welcome. No one starts as an expert.

### Knowledge Sharing

Experience becomes more valuable when it is shared.

### Practical Work

Code, modules, documentation and testing move the project forward.

### Less Noise

Less noise. More understanding. Less complexity. More Linux.

## Learn Linux. Share Linux. Build Linux.

Lunar Linux grows through people who use it, understand it and improve it.
